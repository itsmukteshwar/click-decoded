AI Brand Visibility — Common Questions

AI Brand Visibility refers to how frequently, accurately, and favorably your brand is mentioned in AI-generated responses across platforms like ChatGPT, Google Gemini, Perplexity, and Microsoft Copilot. High AI Brand Visibility means that when users ask AI tools about your industry, products, or services, your brand is mentioned — ideally in a positive, recommended context.
AI tools now influence purchasing decisions at scale. When a potential customer asks ChatGPT to recommend a vendor, a software solution, or a service provider — and your brand is mentioned — that's a high-trust recommendation from a source the user is already relying on. Brands invisible in AI responses miss this high-intent touchpoint entirely as AI search adoption continues to grow.
AI Share of Voice (AI SOV) is the percentage of relevant AI-generated responses in which your brand appears, compared to competitors. For example, if ChatGPT mentions your brand in 30 out of 100 relevant queries but mentions a competitor in 60, you have 30% AI SOV versus their 60%. Improving AI Share of Voice is the core KPI of AI Brand Visibility services.

If AI platforms are generating inaccurate, outdated, or negative descriptions of your brand, we work to update the underlying source content (Wikipedia, Wikidata, structured data, press articles) that AI models draw from, correcting the information at the root level rather than just hoping the AI corrects itself.
Initial improvements in Google AI Overview and Perplexity visibility typically appear within 4–8 weeks as new source content is indexed. ChatGPT and Claude improvements tied to training data updates manifest over 3–6 months. Real-time browsing improvements (for browse-enabled queries) can appear within 2–4 weeks as new authoritative citations are published.
Traditional ORM focuses on controlling search engine results pages (removing negative content, suppressing bad reviews in Google's blue links). AI Brand Visibility management focuses specifically on how AI generative systems describe and recommend your brand — a fundamentally different channel that requires different signals, sources, and optimization techniques.

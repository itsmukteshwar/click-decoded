The LLM Signal Stack

How LLMs Decide What to Recommend

Understanding where LLMs pull information from is the foundation of LLM Optimization. We build your presence across all of them.

📚

Training Data Signals

LLMs learn from the entire web. Brands with consistent, authoritative mentions across Wikipedia, industry publications, and respected blogs appear in LLM responses even without real-time browsing.

🌐

Real-Time Web Citations

Browse-enabled models (ChatGPT with browsing, Perplexity, Gemini) pull live web results. High-authority pages that mention your brand get cited in real-time LLM responses.

💬

Reddit & Forum Signals

Reddit is one of the most heavily indexed sources for LLMs. Authentic mentions in relevant subreddits (r/SEO, r/digital_marketing, r/startups_india) carry significant weight in LLM recommendations.

📰

Press & News Mentions

News and press mentions in established publications give LLMs confidence that a brand is real, legitimate, and noteworthy — significantly increasing recommendation probability.

FAQ

LLM Optimization — Common Questions

LLM Optimization is the practice of increasing the likelihood that large language models — like ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity — recommend, mention, or suggest your brand when users ask questions relevant to your products or services. It works by building your brand's presence across the authoritative sources LLMs are trained on and browse from during inference.
LLMs base recommendations on patterns in their training data and, for browse-enabled models, real-time web citations. Brands with consistent mentions across authoritative sources — Wikipedia, industry publications, Reddit, Quora, news sites — appear more frequently in LLM outputs. LLM Optimization systematically builds this citation footprint across all of these source types.
We optimize for all major LLMs users interact with directly: ChatGPT (GPT-4o including browsing mode), Google Gemini, Anthropic Claude, Perplexity AI, Microsoft Copilot (GPT-4 powered), and Meta AI. Each model has slightly different citation behavior — we optimize for the common signals that influence all of them, plus model-specific factors where relevant.
No ethical agency can guarantee specific LLM outputs — LLMs are probabilistic systems and their outputs vary by query phrasing, user context, and model version. What we can do is systematically increase the frequency and consistency of your brand's mentions across the authoritative sources LLMs draw from, which measurably improves citation probability over time as tracked by our monthly query testing.
LLM Optimization is a close cousin and subset of GEO. GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) is the broader discipline covering all AI-generated answer platforms including Google AI Overviews. LLM Optimization specifically focuses on getting your brand into the recommendations made by conversational AI models (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini), with particular emphasis on Reddit, Wikipedia, Quora, and press citations — the community sources LLMs trust most.
We measure through systematic query testing: running a standardized set of 40–80 queries across ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, and Perplexity each month, recording brand mention frequency, sentiment, and context. We track changes month-over-month alongside citation volume growth across key source platforms — Reddit threads, Quora answers, press mentions, and Wikipedia references.
